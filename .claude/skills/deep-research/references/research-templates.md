# Research Templates by Category

Each section defines search queries and analysis frameworks for a specific market category. When researching, execute at least 3 searches from the relevant template.

---

## Politics

**Search queries:**
1. `"[topic from market title]" polls latest 2026`
2. `"[candidate/issue]" prediction forecast analysis`
3. `"[specific policy/vote]" Congress schedule timeline`
4. `site:fivethirtyeight.com OR site:realclearpolling.com "[topic]"`
5. `"[topic]" prediction market analysis polymarket`

**Analysis framework:**
- What do aggregated polls say? (538, RCP, Nate Silver)
- Is there a trend in recent polls? (momentum matters)
- Are there upcoming events that could shift odds? (debates, primaries, court rulings)
- What do other prediction markets price this at? (Polymarket, PredictIt, Metaculus)
- What's the expert consensus vs. market price?
- Are there known biases? (prediction markets often overweight recent news)

**Red flags:**
- Polling averages vs. market price diverge by >10% — investigate why
- Market is thinly traded (<500 volume) — price may not be informative
- Event is >30 days away — lots of uncertainty, be humble about edge

## Sports

**Search queries:**
1. `"[team A] vs [team B]" odds today`
2. `"[team]" injury report [date]`
3. `"[team A] [team B]" preview analysis [sport]`
4. `"[team]" recent form stats last 10 games`
5. `site:espn.com "[team A]" "[team B]" preview`

**Analysis framework:**
- What are Vegas consensus odds? (compare to Kalshi price)
- Any key injuries or lineup changes? (star player out = massive edge)
- Recent form: last 5-10 games, home/away splits
- Head-to-head record this season
- For spread markets: is the spread moving? Which direction?
- For totals: recent scoring trends, pace of play
- Back-to-back game fatigue? Travel schedule?

**Red flags:**
- Game is already in progress — price is likely efficient, edge is rare
- Line movement against your position — smart money may know something
- Injury status is "questionable" — could go either way right before game

## Crypto

**Search queries:**
1. `bitcoin price analysis today [date]`
2. `"[crypto asset]" whale movements on-chain`
3. `"[crypto asset]" technical analysis support resistance`
4. `crypto market sentiment fear greed index today`
5. `"[crypto asset]" upcoming events catalyst`

**Analysis framework:**
- Current price and 24h trend vs. Kalshi range market boundaries
- Fear & Greed Index (extreme fear = contrarian buy signal, extreme greed = caution)
- Is there an upcoming catalyst? (halving, ETF decision, protocol upgrade, regulatory)
- On-chain metrics: exchange inflows/outflows, whale accumulation
- Macro correlation: S&P 500 trend, DXY strength, rate expectations
- For range markets: where are key support/resistance levels relative to Kalshi thresholds?

**Red flags:**
- High-leverage environment (funding rates extreme) — deleveraging cascades possible
- Regulatory news pending — binary outcome, hard to price
- Weekend market with low liquidity — manipulation risk

## Weather

**Search queries:**
1. `"[city]" weather forecast [date range]`
2. `"[city]" temperature forecast this week [year]`
3. `site:weather.gov "[city]" forecast`
4. `"[weather event]" forecast models GFS ECMWF`
5. `"[city]" climate average [month] historical temperature`

**Analysis framework:**
- NWS official forecast (this is typically what Kalshi settles on for temp markets)
- Model agreement: do GFS and ECMWF agree? (consensus = higher confidence)
- How far out is the forecast? (<3 days = high confidence, >5 days = low)
- Historical base rates: what's the average temperature for this location/date?
- Are there any extreme weather patterns? (El Nino, polar vortex, heat dome)
- For precipitation: model spread is typically wider — lower confidence

**Red flags:**
- Forecast is 5+ days out — accuracy drops significantly
- Models disagree — wait for convergence
- Kalshi settles on official measurement, which may differ from "feels like" temps

## Economics

**Search queries:**
1. `"[indicator]" forecast consensus [date]`
2. `"[indicator]" latest data release [year]`
3. `Federal Reserve FOMC [meeting/minutes] [date]`
4. `"[indicator]" Bloomberg survey median estimate`
5. `BLS CPI report schedule [month] [year]`

**Analysis framework:**
- What's the consensus forecast? (Bloomberg, Reuters surveys)
- What did the indicator read last time? Trend direction?
- Has the Fed signaled anything relevant? (FOMC dots, speeches, minutes)
- Leading indicators: do they point in the same direction?
- Market pricing: what do Treasury yields / fed funds futures imply?
- Release schedule: when does the next data point drop?

**Red flags:**
- Data revisions can change the narrative (previous months often revised)
- Seasonal adjustments can create misleading moves
- Fed communication is deliberately ambiguous — don't over-interpret

## General / Other

**Search queries:**
1. `"[market title keywords]" latest news [year]`
2. `"[topic]" prediction forecast analysis`
3. `"[topic]" expert opinion consensus`
4. `"[event/person]" [specific question from market title]`
5. `site:wikipedia.org "[topic]" — for base rate context`

**Analysis framework:**
- What does a basic web search reveal about the likelihood?
- Are there experts or authorities who have weighed in?
- What's the historical base rate for this type of event?
- Is there any information asymmetry you can exploit?
- Is the market title precise enough to know exactly what settles it?

**Red flags:**
- Market resolution criteria are ambiguous — you might be right but still lose
- Very low volume (<50) — price is probably unreliable
- Event is a one-off with no historical base rate — pure speculation
